How To Repair Laptop Charging Jack

The Laptop is not turning on or the laptop turned on with the help of the battery but the laptop battery is not detecting charging and when you plug in the charger pin and reposition, rotating, or bending the charging pin then the laptop turns on or starts charging the battery. The laptop charging jack is also known as the laptop dc port. This is a common problem due to the damaged or loose charging port/jack inside the laptop. Most laptop power jacks are damaged due to misuse or an accidental fall down. Do you want to know how to repair a charging jack or how to get a laptop charging port, then we are here to help you with that.

The laptop power jack/charging port is the only point through which the laptop gets power supply to operate the laptop different components such as motherboard, hard disk drive, optical drive, screen, and the battery get charging supply. o the laptop charging jack a very important component of a laptop. The laptop charging jack is attached to the motherboard with the help of a connector or directly soldered on the motherboard.

Common Problems of Laptop charging port

There are many types of problems that might be seen with a laptop power jack, you have to identify then go further with fixing as they all have different solutions. Just have a look at the charging port to check it is physically damaged, if it is found physically damaged, then need to replace it with a good quality power jack.

  • 1- The power jack internal pin is damaged.
  • 2- The whole power jack is damaged.
  • 3- The laptop power jack came out of the laptop.
  • 4- The laptop power jock is rotating inside.
  • 5- The motherboard track is burned that connects the jack with the circuit.
  • 6- The laptop jack’s pins are wobbling on the motherboard while connecting the charger pin, and so on.

Tools Needed to Fix Laptop Power Jack

If you want to learn how to fix a laptop power jack, then you require the following tools and materials to repair the DC jack.

  • 1- Soldering iron
  • 2- Multimeter
  • 3- Screw-driver Set
  • 4- New DC jack
  • 5- Desoldering wire
  • 6- Desoldering braid
  • 7- Isopropanol

How to fix laptop power jack without soldering

Fixing a laptop charging jack without soldering is almost not possible in most cases. The laptop’s power jack is directly soldered on the motherboard in that case we need all of the above mentioned tools.
